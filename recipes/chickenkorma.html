Chicken Korma

Recipe Description

This delcious nut and yoghurt based curry is the perfect comfort food for those who love spices! The chicken is marinated and cooked with spices to produce an amazing aroma. Not to spicy or sweet this is one of my favourite recipes!
Recipe from Swasthi's Recipes

Ingredients

  • Marinade
    • 500g Chicken
    • 1/4 teaspoon of ground tumeric
    • 1 tablespoon ginger garlic paste
    • 1 teaspoon of garam masala
    • 1-1.5 tablespoons of chili powder
    • 1/2 teaspoon salt

  • To Saute & Blend
    • 1 1/2 tablespoons of oil
    • 2 green cardamoms
    • 1 cup of red onions sliced (2 medium)
    • 6 cashews
    • 4 almonds
    • 1/4 cup of plain yoghurt or coconut milk

  • For Chicken Korma Gravy
    • 1 tablespoon oil
    • 1 bay leaf
    • 4 cloves
    • 2 inch cinnamon stick
    • 1 cardamon pod
    • 1 to 2 green chilies
    • 1/2 cup of water or as needed
    • 1/4 teaspoon salt
    • 1/2 to 3/4 teaspoon garam masal
    • 1 1/2 teaspoon coriander powder
    • 1/4 teaspoon red chili powder
    • 3 tablespoons of coriander/cilantro chopped

Marinade

  1. Marinate the chicken by mixing well ingredients (1/2 chicken, 3 tablespoons of plain yoghurt and 1 tablespoon ginger garlic paste)
  2. Next add:
    • 1/4 teapsoon tumeric
    • 1 teaspoon red chili powder (or paprika)
    • 1 teaspoon garam masala
    • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  3. Mix all the ingredients together and set aside until needed. Overnight will allow the chicken to absorb the flavours

    4. Make Korma Sauce/Gravy

  4. While the chicken marinates heat oil in a pan and add 2-3 cardamoms
  5. Add sliced onions (2 medium onions) and fry evenly (take care not to burn like I do!) until gloden brown.
  6. Once the onions are caramelized turn off the stove and add 6 cashews and 4 almonds and allow to cool completly
  7. Later add the cooled onions and nuts to a blender jar along with 1/4 cup of yoghurt.
  8. Blend to a smooth paste and add water as needed

    9. Make Chicken Korma

  9. Pour 1-2 tablespoons of oil to the same pan and spread the oil. Add the following whole spices (skip if you don't have them!)
    • 1 bay leaf
    • 3 cloves
    • 2 inch cinnamon stick
  10. Add marinated chicken and slit green chili
  11. Saute on a medium heat until the raw smell of ginger garlic goes away. This takes 5-7 mins.
  12. Add the onion paste. Pour 1/2 cup hot water
  13. Add 1/4 teaspoon salt
  14. Add 1/2 to 3/4 teaspoon garam masala and 1 1/2 teaspoon corriander powder. You can also add some chili powder if you prefer a red coloured korma
  15. Mix well. Bring to a boil then reduce heat. Cover and cook on a medium heat until the chicken turns tender and is soft cooked.
  16. You will see traces of oil over the korma when it's done. Taste test and add more salt as needed. If the korma is too thick add some water to bring it to a good consistency. Spinrle some coriander leaves on top
  17. Serve with rice and roti or naan
